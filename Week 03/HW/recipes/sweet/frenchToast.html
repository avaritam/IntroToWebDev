Hong Kong-Style French Toast

* Recipe taken from Serious Eats *

Brief Description

Hong Kong-style French toast is a decadent, deep-fried or shallow-fried sandwich, often sold in cha chaan tengs, featuring two slices of white bread stuffed with peanut butter or jam, dipped in egg, fried, and topped with butter and condensed milk.

Steps

  1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and preheat oven to 200ºF (93ºC). Arrange bread in a single layer on a wire rack and dry 10 minutes.
  2. Whisk eggs, sugar, and salt in a bowl, then add milk and mix.
  3. Heat oil 2 inches deep to 350ºF (177ºC). Spread peanut butter on 4 slices of bread, top with remaining slices.
  4. Dip sandwiches in egg mixture, allow excess to drip. Fry each sandwich 2 minutes per side until golden. Keep warm on wire rack.
  5. Serve topped with butter and condensed milk.

Ingredients

  • 8 1/2-inch-thick slices milk bread
  • 6 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch kosher salt
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup creamy peanut butter spread
  • Neutral oil for frying
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter for serving
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk for serving
